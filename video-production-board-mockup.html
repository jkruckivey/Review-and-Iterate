Board Structure (Based on Ivey Video Design Framework)

Seven columns across three phases:

  • Pre-Production phase (2 columns): Script & Outline → Outline Review
  • Production phase (2 columns): Slide Design & Assets → Video Assembly
  • Review & QA phase (2 columns): Technical QA → Final Review
  • Done (1 column)

How It Works

Script & Outline: Author drafts the video script, strictly adhering to the "single concept per slide" mapping rule from the framework.

Outline Review: Reviewer checks that the on-screen text does not duplicate the spoken narrative and confirms proper logical branching / slide organization. This prevents building heavy assets for flawed scripts.

Slide Design & Assets: Designers apply the Ivey template styles, fonts, and boundaries. Technical visuals (equations/data) are drafted, and any 4K AI-generated images or B-roll are sourced here.

Video Assembly: The editor brings together the slides, narration/HeyGen footage, applies logical progressive bullet animation (syncing with speech pacing), and standardizes background/music.

Technical QA: A strict check against bugs: Are audio and visuals synchronized? Are progress bars blocking text? Is the VEO watermark removed? Is pacing appropriate at the end?

Final Review: The Learning Designer validates the finished video holistically against the framework goals. Thumbnails (with Ivey logo) are confirmed.

Done: Everything is ready for platform integration.

Quality Non-Negotiables Built In

By enforcing an Outline Review before Slide Design begins, we prevent the "all content appearing at once" or "script paragraphs on slide" mistakes identified in the consistency audits. Separating Technical QA from Final Review catches formatting/glitches distinct from pedagogy issues.
