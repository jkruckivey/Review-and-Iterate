Decades Prior
3M's Innovation Reputation
3M earns a global reputation as one of the world's most innovative companies, sustained by its "30/5 objective."
30% of sales from products under 5 years old
2000
McNerney Takes Over
Former GE executive James McNerney becomes CEO and introduces Six Sigma across the entire organization, including R&D.
2001–2005
Short-Term Gains
Six Sigma delivers measurable efficiency gains. 3M trims its workforce and operating margins grow significantly.
Margins: 17% → 23%
2005
Innovation Stalls
A steep drop in new product launches becomes apparent. Applying process control to R&D appears to be suppressing creative output.
2005
McNerney Departs
McNerney is hired away by Boeing. The damage to 3M's innovation pipeline does not leave with him.
June 2007
BusinessWeek Controversy
BusinessWeek publishes a story blaming Six Sigma for 3M's innovation decline, sparking a wide debate among management thinkers.
The Long Aftermath
3M's innovation never fully recovers. As recently as October 2023, the Wall Street Journal is still writing about 3M's prolonged innovation dry spell.
Still discussed in 2023
Key Question Six Sigma is a powerful tool for managing reliable, repeatable processes. What does 3M's experience suggest about the limits of applying efficiency-focused approaches to innovation?
