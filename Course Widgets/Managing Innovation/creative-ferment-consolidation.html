How Markets Transform Around a Dominant Design

The shift from creative ferment to consolidation reshapes every industry it touches

Stage One
Creative Ferment
No one has a lock on the market. Producers and customers are both experimenting. No single design has yet won the allegiance of the marketplace.

Customers: Uncommitted. Watching. Trying different options. No design has won their allegiance yet.
💡 Competitive advantage goes to firms with the best ideas. Innovation is rapid. The market is wide open.
Stage Two
Consolidation
One design has won. Uncertainty is resolved. The market reorganizes around the dominant design and the firm that owns it.
🏆
The Winner
Owns the standard. Scaling rapidly. Licensing decisions now shape the whole industry.
↘
Firm A

↘
Firm B
Exited or absorbed
🏭
Surviving Firms
Produce similar designs. Compete on cost and scale, not originality.
Dominant design emerges — uncertainty resolved
Firms invest in scaling — manufacturing, distribution, efficiency
Capital beats ideas — advantage shifts to firms that can afford to scale
Market consolidates — fewer firms, similar products, stable structure
👥
Customers: Committed to the standard. Switching costs are now real. The choice has effectively been made.
🔒 Competitive advantage shifts to firms with the capital to scale. Innovation continues, but within the constraints of the dominant design.
