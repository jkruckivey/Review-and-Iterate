Four pitches for creative projects. Vote before you see what they became.

Read each pitch and decide: would you invest? Vote on all four, then reveal what each one actually was.

Vote on all four pitches to reveal the answers.

The point

Probably you recognized these pitches and said you would invest, given the benefit of hindsight. But at the time, people mostly got these wrong. The Blair Witch Project was a passion project by newcomers with no greenlight process applied to it. Star Wars was passed on by United Artists, Universal, and Disney. Fox agreed reluctantly, kept the budget low, and let Lucas keep the merchandising and sequel rights because they considered them worthless. Star Trek was cancelled after three seasons. Cats seemed, to most people, like a very strange idea. The template predicted none of it.
