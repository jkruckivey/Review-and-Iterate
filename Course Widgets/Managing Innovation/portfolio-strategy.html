Two kinds of bets in a creative business

Bet type 1
Original Creative Products
Uncertainty
High
Upside
High
Examples
Blair Witch Project, Star Wars, Cats, Star Trek
The risk
Most lose money. The hits are nearly impossible to predict in advance.
Prediction tools like the Greenlight Process work poorly here. Original ideas tend to look strange by any familiar template.
vs.
Bet type 2
Sequels and IP Tie-Ins
Uncertainty
Lower
Upside
Limited
Examples
Blair Witch 2, Harry Potter videogame adaptations, franchise extensions
The logic
Ride the coattails of an original. Keep production costs low. Make money before audiences realize it is not as good.
Prediction tools work better here. Sequels are not really creative products in the same sense — they are a different business entirely.
The strategy

Place some bets on original ideas, other bets on sequels and IP tie-ins. The originals are highly uncertain but carry potentially great upside. The sequels have limited upside but lower risk, and can offset some of the uncertainty associated with going for the gold with an original idea.
