The Brief
Jordan comes to e-Types
The Brief
Jordan, a Norwegian dental products company, approached e-Types for branding work. The market was loud and cluttered. e-Types proposed doing the opposite: whispering.
The Design
Black, white, and minimal
The Design
Stark, minimal, almost no graphics. The product presented with the quiet authority of a pharmaceutical. It tested brilliantly: customers were more likely to buy and willing to pay more.
The Problem
Not everyone was pleased
The Problem
Jordan's marketing team, whose previous approach the design replaced, compiled a list of tweaks. They secured the new CEO's agreement, then brought the list to e-Types.
The Response
e-Types fires their biggest client
The Response
"We do not tweak our designs." Citing Team Danmark, e-Types refused. Jordan insisted. e-Types fired them: their biggest client, on principle, in August 2008.
The Twist
One month later
The Twist
They felt good about the decision. The only problem was that this was August 2008. One month later, Lehman Brothers filed for bankruptcy and the financial crisis began.
💡 Principled decisions do not always come with good timing. The question is what you do next.
