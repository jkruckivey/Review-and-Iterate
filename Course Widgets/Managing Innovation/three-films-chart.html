Bruce Willis in the lead, but director M. Night Shyamalan was a relative unknown. Studio was more careful: 2,161 screens.

No known stars. $60,000 production budget. An unconventional online marketing campaign listed the actors as either deceased or missing. Studio executives did not know what to make of it: 27 screens.
The Haunting
$177M worldwide
Financial disappointment
Crashed within three weeks. Marketing and distribution costs consumed most returns. Ranks roughly 40th to 50th highest grossing horror film ever.
The Sixth Sense
$673M worldwide
ROI approximately 16 to 17x
Second highest grossing horror film of all time as of 2025. Strong word of mouth and award nominations sustained it for months.
The Blair Witch Project
$278M worldwide
ROI 1,200 to 4,000x
Most profitable film of any genre ever made. Production budget of $60,000 against $278M worldwide gross.
The pattern

The Sixth Sense and The Haunting looked very similar on any category comparison template. One boomed, the other failed. Blair Witch looked like nothing else at all — and became the most profitable film ever made. Truly original ideas look strange and unfamiliar. That is not a bug. It is the point.
