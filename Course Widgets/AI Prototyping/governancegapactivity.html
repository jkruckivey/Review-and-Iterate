Scenario: Governance Gap

A retail company deployed an AI-powered customer service agent six months ago. The agent can answer questions, process returns, and issue refunds up to $50. Recently, they discovered:

  • The agent occasionally issues refunds for products the customer never purchased
  • Refund rates vary significantly across customer demographics
  • No one has formally reviewed the agent's performance since launch
  • The vendor updated the underlying model twice without notifying the company

Your task: Based on what you've learned about AI governance, what went wrong here? What governance gaps does this scenario reveal?

Step 2: Expert Analysis

Compare your initial diagnosis to expert analysis

Here are the five critical governance gaps this scenario reveals:

1. Monitoring and Audit

The Problem: No ongoing performance review means problems went undetected for months.

The Gap: Governance should require regular monitoring of AI system outcomes—not just at launch, but continuously throughout deployment.

2. Fairness Testing

The Problem: Demographic variation in refund rates suggests potential disparate impact.

The Gap: Governance should require disaggregated performance analysis to detect bias across different customer groups.

3. Vendor Management

The Problem: Model updates without notification indicate inadequate vendor governance.

The Gap: Contracts should require notification of material changes; governance should require re-evaluation after updates.

4. Action Boundaries

The Problem: An agent that can issue incorrect refunds may have insufficient guardrails.

The Gap: Governance should define verification requirements for actions with financial impact.

5. Accountability

The Problem: "No one" reviewing the agent suggests unclear ownership.

The Gap: Governance should assign ongoing accountability for deployed systems—someone must be responsible.
