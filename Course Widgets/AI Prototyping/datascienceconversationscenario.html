A Data Science Conversation

Follow a product manager's workflow from raw data to actionable insights

PM
"Summarize this dataset for me—what columns do we have and how many records?"
AI
"This dataset contains 3,450 support tickets with columns: TicketID, CustomerID, Category (5 types: Billing, Technical, Shipping, Account, Other), Priority (Low/Medium/High), ResolutionTime (in hours), SatisfactionScore (1-5), and CreatedDate. Data spans from March 2023 to February 2025."
Why This Matters
Always start by understanding what data you have. This creates shared context between you and the AI for follow-up questions.
PM
"What's the average resolution time by category?"
AI
Runs calculation
"Here are the averages: Billing: 4.2 hours, Technical: 12.7 hours, Shipping: 6.1 hours, Account: 3.8 hours, Other: 8.3 hours. Technical issues take significantly longer to resolve than other categories."
Why This Matters
The AI executes Python code to calculate averages and surfaces a key insight: Technical issues stand out as taking 3x longer than most categories.
PM
"Why is Technical so much higher? Can you show me the distribution?"
AI
Creates histogram
"The distribution shows Technical issues are bimodal—many resolve quickly in 2-4 hours, but there's a second peak at 24+ hours. This suggests some Technical tickets get escalated or stuck, while others are straightforward fixes."
Why This Matters
Follow-up questions dig deeper. The PM doesn't just accept "Technical takes longer"—they ask why and request visualization to understand the pattern.
PM
"Interesting. Can we predict which Technical tickets will take more than 12 hours to resolve?"
AI
Builds classification model
"I built a model to predict long-resolution Technical tickets. It achieves 76% accuracy. The strongest predictors are: Priority level (High priority tickets are more likely to be complex), whether the ticket was created on a weekend (slower initial response), and the customer's account age (newer customers have issues that take longer to diagnose)."
Why This Matters
Moving from descriptive (what happened) to predictive (what will happen). The model reveals which factors drive long resolution times, enabling proactive triage.
PM
"Create a summary for my team meeting—a one-page view with the key findings and that resolution time chart."
AI
Generates PDF
"Here's a summary document with your key findings and visualizations, ready to present."
Why This Matters
Analysis becomes action. The AI packages insights into a presentation-ready deliverable—from question to decision-ready output in one conversation.
What Just Happened The PM went from raw data to actionable insights to a presentation-ready deliverable—all through conversation. No coding, no specialized software, no waiting for an analyst. The AI handled the technical execution; the PM provided the business judgment about what questions to ask and what mattered.
