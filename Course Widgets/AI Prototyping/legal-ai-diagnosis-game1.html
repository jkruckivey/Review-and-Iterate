Harrington Private Wealth: Communication Problems

The AI gives accurate information, but something is clearly off in how it communicates with clients.

Technique Used: RAG only
Status: Failing
AI Response (Actual)
"Hey! So with the Bank of Canada rate change last week, you've basically got a few options for rebalancing. Moving some stuff into shorter-duration bonds is probably the most common thing people do, and also you might want to look at your equity exposure which could be kind of risky right now. Oh and dividend-paying stocks are super relevant here too! Does that help?"
Casual tone ("Hey!", "kind of risky", "super relevant") Vague, imprecise language ("some stuff") No regulatory or compliance framing
What's Interesting

The financial information itself is accurate and current, correctly referencing last week's Bank of Canada rate decision. But the communication style is completely wrong for a wealth management context serving high-net-worth clients.

Incident Report #2

Caldwell Asset Management: Knowledge Problems

The AI communicates perfectly, but portfolio managers are getting nervous about its recommendations.
AI Output: Sample Response Query: ESG disclosure requirements for institutional clients
Client: Caldwell Asset Management
Technique Used: Fine-tuning only
Status: Failing
AI Response (Actual)
"Pursuant to the Canadian Securities Administrators' guidance on ESG-related investment fund disclosure, institutional clients are required to receive documentation outlining the fund's ESG approach, including material risk factors and proxy voting policies. These requirements reflect the CSA's position that ESG disclosure should be consistent with existing continuous disclosure obligations. This framework represents the current standard for institutional ESG reporting in Canada."
References superseded CSA guidance Missing 2024 mandatory climate disclosure rules Knowledge frozen at training cutoff
What's Interesting

The tone is impeccable, the structure is professional, and the regulatory framing sounds authoritative. But the substance is dangerously incomplete: the CSA introduced significantly updated mandatory climate-related disclosure requirements in 2024, and this AI doesn't know that.

Incident Report #3

Hartley & Osei Capital: Something Else Entirely

This firm did everything you recommended. And it still isn't working.
AI Output: Sample Response Query: Suitability assessment for alternative investments
Client: Hartley & Osei Capital
Technique Used: Fine-tuning + RAG
Status: Failing
AI Response (Actual)
"Pursuant to applicable suitability obligations, alternative investments require assessment of client risk tolerance, liquidity needs, and investment horizon. As noted in our Q3 internal investment committee memo, alternative allocations above 20% require additional sign-off. Per the portfolio review conducted March 2023, clients in the growth category are generally suitable for moderate alternative exposure, subject to advisor discretion."
Cites internal memo as regulatory authority References internal portfolio review as industry standard Training data quality issue
What the Firm Tells You

They used fine-tuning with RAG exactly as recommended. But when building their fine-tuning dataset, they pulled everything from their internal document server: internal investment committee memos, advisor emails, draft client letters, and internal policy documents, alongside actual regulatory guidance. The RAG knowledge base was built from the same mixed source.

⚠️ This one is different. The technique combination is right. Something else went wrong. Can you spot it?

MediCom Apply Challenge
The pattern across all three incidents: Choosing the right technique is about understanding what each one actually controls. Fine-tuning shapes how the model behaves: its voice, tone, terminology, and structure. RAG shapes what the model knows at the moment of a query. These are distinct jobs. And as Hartley & Osei Capital showed, even the right technique choice fails without rigorous data behind it.
