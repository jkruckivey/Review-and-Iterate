Phase 1

Launch

High enthusiasm — momentum feels unstoppable

Characteristics
  • People energized by the vision
  • Investment committed, teams assembled
  • Strong senior sponsorship visible
Risk
Unrealistic expectations The gap between expectation and reality fuels later disillusionment.
Leadership Actions
  • Set honest expectations about the messy middle ahead
  • Establish early win milestones to anchor momentum
  • Build governance structures before you need them
Phase 2

Reality Sets In

First obstacles emerge — progress stalls

Characteristics
  • Integration problems surface
  • Capability gaps become visible
  • Political resistance emerges
  • Timeline slippage begins
Risk
Eroding enthusiasm Quick wins prove elusive and early supporters grow frustrated.
Leadership Actions
  • Communicate obstacles transparently
  • Celebrate any wins, however small
  • Reaffirm commitment publicly and visibly
Phase 3

The Messy Middle

Energy lowest, doubt peaks — most fail here

Three Forces Converge
  • Initial optimism fades as reality proves slower and messier
  • Hidden obstacles surface during execution
  • Competing priorities pull attention away
Risk
Transformation abandoned Not because the vision was wrong, but because leadership didn't sustain momentum.
Leadership Actions
  • Protect transformation resources fiercely
  • Refresh the "why" — reconnect people to purpose
  • Model resilience; your tone sets organizational tone
  • Address obstacles transparently, not defensively
Phase 4

Traction

Results visible, skeptics convert

Characteristics
  • Early adopters succeed and share stories
  • Evidence accumulates; skeptics convert
  • Momentum rebuilds and accelerates
  • Transformation becomes self-sustaining
Note
Reached only by navigating the valley Organizations that panic in Phase 3 never reach this stage.
Leadership Actions
  • Document and share success stories broadly
  • Codify what worked into organizational practices
  • Shift from active pushing to shaping and steering
