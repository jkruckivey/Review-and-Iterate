Background Scenario

▼

Context: Arthur Rojas is a first-year HBA student at Ivey Business School who had finished his first term and was actively pursuing summer job opportunities in investment banking. To practice for interviews, he approached his friend Shaun Ramanatt, a second-year HBA who had spent the previous summer working at a prestigious investment bank in Toronto. After 15 minutes of resume-based questioning, Shaun presented this challenge:

"Every company is different. Every company is managed in a different way and meets with varying degrees of financial success. However, the industry in which a given firm operates will invariably have an impact upon its capital needs, financing abilities and overall financial performance. Through whatever analysis you deem necessary, match each balance sheet with its respective industry, and explain your rationale for doing so. You have five minutes."

Assignment 1: Match the eight companies (A-H) to their industries based on distinctive asset structures and financial ratio patterns. Consider asset composition (PP&E%, inventory, intangibles), capital structure (debt ratios, equity), liquidity (current ratio), and operational efficiency (asset turnover, days receivables).

Optional Extension (Assignment 2): After matching, you can further analyze these companies by plotting XY scatter diagrams comparing: (1) Total Debt/Total Assets vs. EBITDA Margin, and (2) Net Sales/Total Assets vs. Plant & Equipment percentage. This visualization reveals industry clustering patterns.

Matching Hints

▼
Capital-intensive industries (utilities, mining, REITs) have HIGH PP&E%
Service industries (consulting, banking, non-profits) have LOW PP&E%
Retailers have significant inventory
Consulting firms have high receivables (bill for time) and high intangibles (people = goodwill)
Banks have very different balance sheets (loans = assets, deposits = liabilities)
Non-profits have little/no equity (negative retained earnings often)
Tech startups burn cash, have negative retained earnings, and high leverage
