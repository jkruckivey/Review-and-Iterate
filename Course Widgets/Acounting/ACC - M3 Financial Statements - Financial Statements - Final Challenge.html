The Business: Marco's Pizza Kitchen

Background Story

Owner: Marco Rodriguez

Business Type: Small pizza restaurant specializing in artisanal pizzas

Location: Downtown neighborhood location

Opening Date: March 1, 2025

Your Role: As Marco's accountant, you'll prepare his first month's financial statements (March 2025)

Marco opened his dream restaurant on March 1st. He borrowed money from the bank, purchased equipment, bought food supplies, generated sales, paid expenses, and now needs to understand his financial position. Let's analyze all of March's transactions and build both financial statements.

Build the Income Statement

Calculate Marco's profitability for March 2025

Instructions

  • Identify all revenue transactions (when was performance delivered?)
  • Calculate Cost of Goods Sold (food inventory actually used)
  • List all operating expenses (rent, wages, utilities, advertising, depreciation)
  • Include interest expense (cost of borrowing)
  • Include income tax expense (tax on profits)
  • Calculate subtotals: Gross Profit, Operating Income, Income Before Tax, Net Income

Marco's Pizza Kitchen

Income Statement

For the Month Ended March 31, 2025

Revenues

Hint: Revenue is recognized when goods/services are delivered, not when cash is received. Look for all sales transactions (both cash and credit). Add March 10 sales + March 20 sales.

Cost of Goods Sold

Hint: COGS is the direct cost of inventory used to produce goods sold. Don't confuse "purchased" with "used". Add the food used on March 15 + food used on March 22.

Operating Expenses

Hint: Operating expenses are costs of running the business (not producing goods). Look for: rent paid, wages paid, utilities paid, advertising paid, and depreciation recorded.

Non-Operating Items

Hint: Interest expense is the cost of borrowing money. Look for the March 31 accrued interest transaction.

Hint: Income tax expense is based on profits before tax. Look for the March 31 tax transaction.

Build the Balance Sheet

Show Marco's financial position as of March 31, 2025

Instructions

  • Calculate ending Cash (start with investments + loan, then add/subtract all cash transactions)
  • Determine Accounts Receivable (sales made on credit not yet collected)
  • Calculate ending Inventory (food purchased minus food used)
  • Calculate net Equipment (cost minus accumulated depreciation)
  • List Long-Term Debt (bank loan)
  • Show Owner's Capital (initial investment)
  • Show Retained Earnings (this is Net Income from the Income Statement!)

Marco's Pizza Kitchen

Balance Sheet

As of March 31, 2025

ASSETS

Current Assets

Hint: Start with owner investment ($25,000) + bank loan ($30,000) = $55,000. Then subtract: equipment purchase, inventory purchase, and all cash expenses paid. Add: all cash collections from sales.

Hint: Sales made on credit but not yet collected. Look at March 20 sales - some were on credit to catering clients.

Hint: Food purchased ($4,500) minus food used ($2,100 + $2,400). The remaining food is still in inventory.

Non-Current Assets

Hint: Equipment cost = $18,000. Accumulated depreciation = total depreciation recorded to date ($150 for March).

LIABILITIES

Current Liabilities

Hint: Interest accrued but not yet paid. This is the interest expense from March 31.

Hint: Tax expense recorded but not yet paid. This is the income tax expense from March 31.

Non-Current Liabilities

Hint: The original loan amount borrowed on March 2.

EQUITY

Hint: Marco's initial investment on March 1.

Hint: This is where Net Income from the Income Statement flows! Beginning Retained Earnings = $0 (new business) + Net Income - Dividends ($0). So Retained Earnings = Net Income.
