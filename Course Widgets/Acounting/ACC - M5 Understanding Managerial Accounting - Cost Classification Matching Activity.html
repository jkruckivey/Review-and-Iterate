Cost Classification Reference Guide

Review these detailed explanations and examples to deepen your understanding of cost classifications.

1. Fixed vs. Variable Costs (Cost Behaviour)

1. Fixed Costs

Total amount stays constant regardless of activity level (within relevant range).

Per-unit amount decreases as volume increases.

Examples: Rent, salaries, insurance, depreciation, property taxes

2. Variable Costs

Total amount changes in direct proportion to activity level.

Per-unit amount stays constant regardless of volume.

Examples: Direct materials, direct labor, sales commissions, shipping costs

3. Mixed Costs (Semi-Variable)

Contain both fixed and variable components.

Examples: Utility bills (base charge + usage), phone bills, equipment rental (base fee + per-use charge)

Example: Manufacturing 1,000 vs. 2,000 Units

Rent (Fixed Cost):

1,000 units: Total = $10,000 | Per unit = $10

2,000 units: Total = $10,000 | Per unit = $5

Direct Materials (Variable Cost):

1,000 units: Total = $5,000 | Per unit = $5

2,000 units: Total = $10,000 | Per unit = $5

2. Direct vs. Indirect Costs (Traceability)

1. Direct Costs

Can be easily and accurately traced to a specific cost object (product, department, project).

Examples: Direct materials (wood in a table), direct labor (assembly worker's time), dedicated equipment

2. Indirect Costs (Overhead)

Cannot be easily traced to a specific cost object. Must be allocated using some basis.

Examples: Factory rent, utilities, supervisory salaries, equipment depreciation, supplies

3. Product vs. Period Costs (Inventory Treatment)

1. Product Costs (Inventoriable Costs)

Attached to inventory as an asset on the balance sheet.

Become Cost of Goods Sold (expense) when the product is sold.

Include: Direct materials, direct labor, manufacturing overhead

2. Period Costs

Expensed immediately in the period incurred (not attached to inventory).

Include: Selling expenses (advertising, sales salaries, commissions) and administrative expenses (executive salaries, office rent, accounting)
