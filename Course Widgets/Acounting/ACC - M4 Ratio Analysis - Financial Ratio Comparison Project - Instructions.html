Financial Ratio Comparison Project Instructions

Apply ratio analysis skills to real-world investment decisions

Project Purpose

Apply your ratio analysis skills to compare two real companies in the same industry. You'll calculate ratios, interpret patterns, and make an investment recommendation.

Your Role: Comparative Financial Analyst

Scenario: You're a financial analyst at Sterling Investment Partners. Your manager asks you to compare two companies in the same industry and recommend which is the better investment based on financial ratio analysis.

Your Task: Choose two public companies in the same industry (e.g., Coca-Cola vs. PepsiCo; Nike vs. Adidas; Apple vs. Microsoft; Walmart vs. Target) and perform a comparative ratio analysis.

2 Calculate and Compare Key Ratios

Calculate a minimum of 8 ratios from the following categories:

Liquidity Ratios

  • Current Ratio Current Ratio = Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities
  • Acid Test Ratio Acid Test Ratio = (Cash + Receivables) ÷ Current Liabilities

Profitability Ratios

  • Gross Profit Margin Gross Profit Margin = (Gross Profit ÷ Revenues) × 100%
  • Net Profit Margin Net Profit Margin = (Net Income ÷ Revenues) × 100%
  • Return on Assets (ROA) Return on Assets = (Net Income ÷ Total Assets) × 100%
  • Return on Equity (ROE) Return on Equity = (Net Income ÷ Equity) × 100%

Leverage Ratios

  • Debt-to-Assets Ratio Debt-to-Assets = Total Debt ÷ Total Assets

Efficiency Ratios

  • Asset Turnover Asset Turnover = Net Sales ÷ Total Assets

3 Write Your Comparative Analysis Report

Report Structure 400-600 words

  1. Executive Summary
    • Companies compared and industry
    • One-sentence recommendation
  2. Liquidity Analysis
    • Compare current ratios and acid test ratios
    • Which company has better short-term liquidity? Why does this matter?
  3. Profitability Analysis
    • Compare profit margins (gross, net), ROA, and ROE
    • Which company is more profitable? What might explain the difference?
  4. Leverage Analysis
    • Compare debt-to-assets ratios
    • Which company uses more financial leverage? Is this a concern?
  5. Efficiency Analysis
    • Compare asset turnover
    • Which company uses assets more efficiently?
  6. Investment Recommendation
    • Based on your ratio analysis, which company appears to be the better investment?
    • What are the key strengths and weaknesses of each?
    • What additional information would you want before making a final decision?
