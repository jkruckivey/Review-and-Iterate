Luna's Candle Co. - Building the Balance Sheet

Understanding Financial Position at January 31

Let's continue with Luna's Candle Co. from the previous module. We calculated her net income was $1,650. Now let's see her complete financial position at January 31.

Assets at January 31:

Cash

  • Starting balance (owner investment): +$15,000
  • Purchased supplies: –$3,500
  • Collected from sales: +$4,800
  • Paid rent: –$800
  • Paid wages: –$1,200
  • Paid utilities: –$150
  • Paid advertising: –$300
Ending Cash = $13,850

Accounts Receivable

Sales made on credit (not yet collected): $1,400

Inventory (Supplies for candle-making)

  • Purchased supplies: $3,500
  • Used for producing candles sold: –$2,100
Ending Inventory = $1,400
Total Assets

$13,850 (Cash) + $1,400 (Accounts Receivable) + $1,400 (Inventory)

= $16,650

Liabilities at January 31

Luna has no unpaid bills or outstanding loans.

Total Liabilities
= $0

Equity at January 31

Owner's Capital

Initial investment: $15,000

Retained Earnings

  • Beginning Retained Earnings: $0 (new business)
  • Plus: Net Income for January: +$1,650
  • Less: Dividends/withdrawals: $0
Ending Retained Earnings = $1,650
Total Equity

$15,000 (Owner's Capital) + $1,650 (Retained Earnings)

= $16,650
