Timeline: The Department Store Collapse
1990s
Peak Dominance

Sears was America's largest retailer. Department stores anchored every mall.

2000s
Walmart Rises

Discount retailers improve quality while maintaining price advantage. Middle-market customers defect.

2010s
Amazon & Fast Fashion

Online shopping offers infinite selection. H&M/Zara offer trendy fashion at mid-market prices.

2018
Sears Bankruptcy

132-year-old retailer files Chapter 11. At its peak, employed around 350,000 in its retail operations.

2020
JCPenney Bankruptcy

118-year-old retailer files Chapter 11. COVID accelerates long decline.

2024
Survivors Still Struggling

Macy's and Kohl's remain in business but continue closing stores and fighting for relevance.

The Problem

Customers couldn't articulate WHY they'd shop at Sears. Not cheapest, not trendiest, not most convenient.

The Cause

Strategic drift: gradual loss of distinctiveness as competitors improved from all directions.

The Lesson

Mid-market positions require constant vigilance. Pick a direction or get squeezed.

Key Insight

Without clear differentiation, mid-market companies drift toward irrelevance. Customers defect to whoever offers the best value—whether that's better fashion, lower prices, or greater convenience.
