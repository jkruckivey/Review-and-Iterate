Five Forces Industry Analyzer

Evaluate industry attractiveness using Porter's Five Forces framework

How to use: Rate each competitive force from 1-5 based on its strength in your chosen industry. Higher force strength means more competitive pressure and lower industry profitability. Use the diagnostic questions to guide your assessment.

1. Threat of New Entry

Rate this force: Medium (3)
Low Threat High Threat

Consider these factors:

  • Are there high capital requirements to enter this industry?
  • Do incumbent firms have strong brand loyalty?
  • Are there significant economies of scale?
  • How difficult is regulatory approval?
  • Do incumbents control critical resources or distribution channels?

2. Supplier Bargaining Power

Rate this force: Medium (3)
Low Power High Power

Consider these factors:

  • How many suppliers exist in the market?
  • Can suppliers easily forward integrate into your industry?
  • How critical are their inputs to your product quality?
  • Are there switching costs between suppliers?
  • Do suppliers serve multiple industries or just yours?

3. Buyer Bargaining Power

Rate this force: Medium (3)
Low Power High Power

Consider these factors:

  • How concentrated are buyers compared to sellers?
  • Can buyers easily backward integrate?
  • How price-sensitive are buyers?
  • Do buyers purchase in large volumes?
  • Is product differentiation low (commodity-like)?

4. Threat of Substitutes

Rate this force: Medium (3)
Low Threat High Threat

Consider these factors:

  • Are there alternative solutions to customer needs?
  • What's the price-performance of substitute products?
  • How easy is it for customers to switch to substitutes?
  • Are substitutes from different industries or technologies?
  • Is the substitute trend growing or declining?

5. Rivalry Among Existing Competitors

Rate this force: Medium (3)
Low Rivalry High Rivalry

Consider these factors:

  • How many competitors exist in the industry?
  • Is industry growth high or low/declining?
  • Are fixed costs high (creating pressure to maintain volume)?
  • Is product differentiation low (leading to price competition)?
  • Are exit barriers high (keeping firms in despite losses)?
