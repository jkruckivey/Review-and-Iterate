Step 1: Investigate Customer Interviews

A fast-food chain wants to increase milkshake sales. They've tried everything: making it thicker, adding flavors, changing the price. Nothing works consistently. You're hired to figure out why customers buy milkshakes.

Click each interview to learn about different customers:

Morning Commuter (7:30am)

"I buy a milkshake at 7am for my long drive to work. It keeps me full until lunch and gives me something to do during my boring commute. I tried bananas, but they're gone in 3 bites. Bagels are too messy. The milkshake lasts 20 minutes and is entertaining to drink."

Afternoon Parent (3:30pm)

"I buy a milkshake after school to make my kid happy without spoiling dinner. It's substantial enough to satisfy them but not as bad as candy. It also keeps them quiet in the car for a few minutes."

Evening Treat-Seeker (8:00pm)

"I buy a milkshake when I want to indulge myself but don't want to commit to a full dessert. It feels substantial and takes time to drink, so it's more satisfying than a cookie but less guilt than ice cream."
