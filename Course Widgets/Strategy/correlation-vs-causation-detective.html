The Correlation vs. Causation Problem

Traditional market research often identifies correlations (who buys) but fails to explain causation (why they buy). Demographic patterns like "married customers buy more milkshakes" don't reveal the underlying job the product does.

The Jobs-to-Be-Done framework solves this by asking: "What job did you hire that milkshake to do?" This shifts focus from customer characteristics to the progress they're trying to make. In the next part of this module, you'll discover how observational research revealed the true jobs customers hire milkshakes for - insights that transform strategy.
