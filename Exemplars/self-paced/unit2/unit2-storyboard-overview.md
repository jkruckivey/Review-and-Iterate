**Self-Paced Course Structure**
**Platform Week:** 2

---

## Unit Narrative Arc

Unit 2 teaches that **realistic goal setting requires understanding industry constraints**. The narrative unfolds across four interconnected modules:

### The Journey

**Module 1: "What Mountain Do You Want to Climb?"**
- *Hook:* Mountain metaphor for industries and competitive positions
- *Data:* Industry performance data (ROA, Tobin's Q, 30-year comparison)
- *Insight:* Industry structure explains ~20% of performance variation
- *Outcome:* Students understand that realistic goals depend on industry attractiveness

**Module 2: "The Five Forces That Shape Industry Attractiveness"**
- *Foundation:* Porter's Five Forces framework (rivalry, new entrants, substitutes, suppliers, customers)
- *Method:* Diagnostic questions for each force
- *Examples:* Airlines (intense rivalry), pharma (high barriers to entry), software (network effects)
- *Outcome:* Students can analyze any industry using structured framework

**Module 3: "Apply Five Forces to Real Industries and Companies"**
- *Practice:* Interactive scenario analysis (8 different industries)
- *Depth:* Five Forces analysis dashboard (rate each force, see industry attractiveness)
- *Real-world:* Canary Medical CEO interview on medical device industry forces
- *Application:* Guided reflection—analyze an industry of their choice
- *Outcome:* Students demonstrate competency through independent analysis

**Module 4: "Synthesize and Look Forward"**
- *Consolidation:* Key takeaways summary
- *Readings:* Magretta and Porter original sources for depth
- *Bridge:* Connect goal-setting to next unit (strategic positioning)
- *Preview:* "Next: Where can we position ourselves within this industry?"

## Interactive Element Strategy

### Widgets (3 total across unit)

1. **Five Forces Identifier** (Module 3)
   - 8 real-world scenarios (Uber, Netflix, Tesla, etc.)
   - Students select which force is most relevant
   - Instant feedback with strategic implications
   - Time: 8 minutes
   - Learning: Recognize forces in practice

2. **Five Forces Analyzer** (Module 3)
   - 5 sliders (1-5 scale for each force)
   - Real-time dashboard showing industry attractiveness
   - Diagnostic questions for each force
   - Comparison to other industries
   - Time: 8 minutes
   - Learning: Rate forces quantitatively, synthesize into overall assessment

3. **Industry Performance Explorer** (Module 1)
   - Interactive visualization of ROA and Tobin's Q
   - Toggle between industries (Pharma, Software, Airlines, etc.)
   - Toggle between 1980 and 2010 data
   - Insights: "Which industries are most attractive? Why?"
   - Time: 5 minutes
   - Learning: See industry effects empirically

### Knowledge Checks (Formative, 0 points)

- Module 1: True/False about industry effects (2 min, 1 question)
- Module 2: Identify five forces from scenarios (5 min, 5 questions)
- Module 3: Reflection with AI coach feedback (20 min, guided analysis)
- Module 4: Bridge reflection (5 min, 1 question)

### Assessment Approach

- **No high-stakes exams**
- **All assessments are formative** (feedback-focused, no points)
- **Widgets provide instant feedback** (transparent answer keys)
- **AI Coach provides reflection feedback** (rubric-based, personalized)
- **Encourage error as learning** (widgets let students try multiple times)
- **Preparation for Unit 10 capstone** (all skills build toward final project)

## Accessibility & Universal Design

### WCAG 2.2 AA Compliance

All elements include:
- **Video captions** — VTT files for all 4 instructor videos + interview
- **Alt text** — Descriptive labels for all infographics and diagrams
- **Keyboard navigation** — All widgets fully keyboard operable (Tab, Enter, arrows)
- **Screen reader support** — ARIA labels on all interactive elements
- **High contrast** — 4.5:1 minimum contrast ratio on text
- **Focus indicators** — Visible 3px outline on all focusable elements
- **Responsive design** — Works on mobile (6-inch screens) through desktop (27-inch monitors)

### Universal Design for Learning (UDL)

- **Multiple means of representation:** Text + video + interactive visualization + infographics
- **Multiple means of engagement:** Choice of industry for final reflection; multiple entry points via examples
- **Multiple means of expression:** Written reflection; interactive selection; discussion

## Self-Paced Adaptations & Why

The self-paced format makes intentional changes from cohort:

| Cohort Element | Self-Paced Change | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 8 modules (Bridge, Welcome, Executive, Core, Interactive, Case, Assessment, Wrap-Up) | 4 modules (Intro, Content, Practice, Wrap-Up) | Self-paced learners don't need social scaffolding or synchronous cohort rituals |
| Executive interview as primary learning | Instructor video + interview clips | Reduces cognitive load; adds voice of instruction |
| 6-8 widgets per unit | 2-3 widgets per module | Deeper practice on fewer concepts vs. coverage |
| Weekly assessments + anchor project tracking | Formative checks + final capstone project | No need for weekly momentum checks; final project creates coherence |
| 7-9 hours per week | 4.5–5.5 hours per unit | Self-paced students can take longer; time estimates allow autonomy |

## Notes for Implementation Team

### For Video Producer
- Target audience: Professionals returning to learning; assumes no strategy background
- Tone: Conversational but authoritative; use real examples (airlines, pharma)
- Graphics: Simple animated diagrams (Five Forces model, mountain metaphor, ROA chart)
- Avoid: Jargon without explanation; overly academic tone; excessive length

### For Widget Developer
- All widgets must follow Uplimit design system (Geist font, neutral grays, 1px borders)
- Keyboard navigation: Tab through all inputs, Enter to submit, Arrows to adjust sliders
- Mobile: Test on 6-inch phone screen; responsive layout
- Accessibility: ARIA labels, high contrast, focus indicators
- Error states: Clear explanations if student selects incorrect answer

### For Content Editor
- Simplify industry examples (airlines, pharma, software) for consistency
- Link back to Module 0 strategy framework four-part model
- Establish Canary Medical as running case (appears in Modules 3, 5, 6, 7, 9)
- Watch tone: Balance rigor with accessibility

### For Assessment Designer
- Knowledge checks are formative (no points, no grades)
- Feedback should be non-judgmental ("Not quite—here's why...")
- Rubric for reflection: 1) Identified all 5 forces, 2) Rated forces 1-5, 3) Synthesized into industry attractiveness, 4) Set realistic goal
- Allow multiple attempts (widgets should let students try again without penalty)
