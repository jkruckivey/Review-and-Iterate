## Unit Overview

Unit 2 introduces the first element of the four-part strategy framework: **Goal Setting**. Students learn that achieving realistic goals requires understanding industry-level constraints. Industry structure—captured through Porter's Five Forces—explains approximately 20% of company performance variation.

The unit answers the central question: **"What mountain can you actually climb?"** rather than "How high do you want to climb?" This corrects a common strategic error: setting implausibly high goals divorced from industry realities.

### Learning Objectives

By completing Unit 2, students will be able to:

- **Comprehend** why industry structure constrains achievable profitability goals
- **Analyze** any industry using Porter's Five Forces framework
- **Identify** the strongest competitive forces in a given industry
- **Apply** Five Forces analysis to realistic goal-setting scenarios
- **Compare** profitability levels across industries and explain structural differences

### Strategic Context

This unit maps to the self-paced platform's 4-module structure:

1. **Module 1: Introduction to Goal Setting** — Learning outcomes, mountain metaphor, industry data
2. **Module 2: Five Forces Framework** — Rivalry, substitutes, new entrants, suppliers, customers
3. **Module 3: Practice and Application** — Interactive analyzer, Canary Medical case, reflection
4. **Module 4: Wrap-Up** — Readings, key takeaways, bridge to next unit

**Total Time:** 4.5–5.5 hours

The self-paced format differs from the cohort 8-module structure in key ways:

| **Modules per unit** | 8 | 4 |
| **Module flow** | Bridge → Welcome → Executive → Core → Interactive → Case → Assessment → Wrap-Up | Intro → Content → Practice → Wrap-Up |
| **Video approach** | Executive interviews as primary learning | Instructor videos + interview clips |
| **Assessment** | Weekly micro-assessments + anchor project milestone | Formative quizzes + reflection |
| **Pacing** | Synchronized cohort beats | Self-determined |

### Content Organization

**Module 1: Introduction to Goal Setting** focuses on establishing why goal-setting matters:
- Hook: "What mountain do you want to climb?" (mountain metaphor)
- Data: Industry performance visualization (ROA, Tobin's Q, 1980 vs. 2010)
- Framework preview: Four-part strategy model with goal as first element
- Learning outcomes checklist (interactive progress tracker)

**Module 2: Five Forces Framework** teaches the analytical foundation:
- Knowledge check (identify five forces from intro text)
- Rivalry deep dive with airline example
- New entrants, suppliers, customers frameworks
- Barriers to entry (supply-side vs. demand-side)
- Summary connecting forces to industry attractiveness

**Module 3: Practice and Application** builds competency through guided practice:
- Five Forces Identifier widget (8 scenarios, instant feedback)
- Five Forces Analyzer widget (slider-based force rating)
- Canary Medical video interview (CEO Bill Hunter on industry forces)
- Reflection: Analyze industry of choice using Five Forces framework

**Module 4: Wrap-Up** synthesizes learning and bridges forward:
- Recommended readings (Magretta & Porter)
- Key takeaways summary
- Bridge to Unit 3: Value Proposition & Strategic Positioning

## Design Principles

### 1. **Self-Paced Autonomy**
Students can progress at their own pace without cohort pressure. Each module is self-contained but builds deliberately toward unit mastery. Time estimates are provided for planning.

### 2. **Interactive-First Pedagogy**
Reading is minimized (30-40% of learning), with 60-70% driven by:
- Interactive widgets (practice scenarios, analysis tools)
- Executive interviews (real strategic thinking)
- Guided reflection (application to personal contexts)

### 3. **Canary Medical Throughout**
The real-time case study of Canary Medical (a Vancouver medical-device company) appears in:
- Module 3: CEO interview on industry forces
- Modules 5-9: Continued as application example
- Creates coherent narrative across units

### 4. **Bounded Choices, Clear Feedback**
Rather than open-ended exercises, widgets use bounded choices (5-10 options) with instant, non-judgmental feedback explaining why answers are correct/incorrect. This accelerates learning.

### 5. **Formative Assessment Embedded**
No high-stakes exams. Instead, low-stakes formative checks throughout:
- Knowledge checks after concept introduction
- Practice quizzes after widget use
- Guided reflection at module close
- All prepare for Unit 10 capstone project
