The "Agile" Enhancements

To support running six concurrent courses using the standardized review integration from Phase 3, we've updated the traditional Kanban board into a specialized Agile layout:

1. Horizontal Swimlanes (Macro-Visibility)

Instead of burying *Fundamentals of Analytics* units inside a massive 20-card "Development" column, swimlanes give Course Leads immediate clarity on exactly where their specific course is stuck. The board is now read Left-to-Right for flow, and Top-to-Bottom for cross-team resource allocation.

2. WIP Limits (Forcing Resolution)

Notice the Platform QA (QA 2) column header is red. It has a Work-In-Progress limit of 2, but currently holds 3 items. This visually signals to the entire production team that QA is the bottleneck. The rule is: Developers stop pulling cards from "Source Material" and instead swarm the QA column to help clear the backlog.

3. The "Blocked" Parking Lot (Hatched Red Stylization)

Look at Week 2: Sports Mktg. It is blocked waiting on an SME for answer keys. Instead of just blending in with a purple label, the hatched red background acts as a warning beacon during project stand-ups. It cannot be ignored.

4. Complexity Badges (Triage Indicators)

On the top right of each card, there is an icon (🎥 for HeyGen Video heavy, 📄 for Text/Widget heavy, 🔢 for Equation formatting). This solves the QA triage problem: A reviewer searching for a task can see that the 🎥 card will require 30 minutes of deep audio/visual synchronization checking, whereas the 📄 card is a quick CSS accessibility review.
