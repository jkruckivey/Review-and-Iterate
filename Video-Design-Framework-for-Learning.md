## ****Purpose****

* Provide a framework for designing video slides and templates that support effective learning
* Intended for:
  + Learning Experience Designers
  + Learning Experience Specialists
  + Content Creators
  + Video Editors
* Goal: Create videos that are clear, engaging, consistent, and optimized for learning.

## ****Non-Negotiables****

* Each slide must communicate a single concept
* All visuals must be directly referenced or explained in the narration
* On-screen text should not duplicate spoken content
* Bullet points must be revealed progressively to support pacing
* Visual elements must clearly support the concept being taught

## ****Core Principles****

* **Aesthetic, but purposeful**
  + Slides should be visually polished, with each element serving a clear instructional purpose
  + Visual elements that do not enhance understanding should be removed
  + Exception: Technical visuals (e.g., equations, formulas, data) should be retained when essential to the explanation
* Visuals should support—not replicate—narration
* Prioritize clarity over visual complexity
* Consistency across slides and videos reinforces learner confidence

## ****Content & Topic Rules****

**Topics**

* One topic or theme per video
* Do not use section pages or dividers
* Use logical branching templates when required
* Do not use “etc.” in examples

**Course References**

* Use only “this week”
* Do not reference specific weeks (e.g., week 3, 4)
* Do not reference modules or units
* Only reference previous topics
* Only reference weeks (cohort) and units (self-paced) in the Getting Started video

**Layout Rules**

* Each slide should present a single, clearly defined idea
* Keep text concise (8–12 words per line recommended)
* Establish clear visual hierarchy (headline → supporting content → visual)
* Maintain consistent spacing and alignment throughout
* Avoid overloading slides with excessive content

**Shapes, Icons & Visual Structure**

* Icons must be paired with headings (no standalone icons)
* Use Logic branding when required
* Similar-sized shapes must represent equal value
* Use borders (outlined rectangles) to emphasize key content
* Do not overuse boxes

## ****Typography, Style & Writing Standards****

**Writing & Language**

* Use “and” instead of “&”
* Use “vs.” (with a period)
* Do not use “=” unless in formulas
* Keep wording concise and conversational

**Capitalization & Grammar**

* Capitalize major words in headings (not “and,” “the,” etc.)
* Do not use ALL CAPS (except infographics)
* Numbers under 10 must be spelled out
* Use consistent Canadian spelling
* Be consistent with abbreviations (e.g., R&D vs R and D)
* Run text through Grammarly before finalizing

**Bullet Point Standards**

* Use bullet points to organize information; avoid paragraph-style text
* Keep bullet points concise (ideally one line each)
* Limit to 3–5 bullet points per slide
* Use consistent bullet styling (solid circle)
* Avoid semicolons or periods in standard lists

**Numbering Rules**

* If faculty references specific numbers, use numbered lists
* If not, use bullet points

**Special Cases**

* B-roll or image-based slides may not require bullet text
* Include numbering if referenced in narration

**Emphasis Rules**

* Use **bold** for emphasis (preferred)
* Use italics for proper nouns
* Apply emphasis styles sparingly and consistently

**Fonts, Colors & Sizing**

* Follow Ivey standards
* Maintain consistent font sizes across all slides
* Prioritize primary colors over secondary or tertiary options
* **All font styles, sizes, and color standards are predefined in the template and should be used as-is**

**Alignment & Spacing**

* Titles: left-aligned
* Bullets/tables: left-aligned
* Do not justify or right-align text
* Maintain consistent vertical alignment across all slides
* Use one space after bullets/periods
* Use single spacing between bullets

## ****Animation & Emphasis****

**Core Animation Rules**

* Use simple, consistent animations across all slides
* Introduce content in a logical sequence (top-to-bottom, left-to-right)
* Avoid animations that distract from the learning objective

**Bullet & Content Animation**

* Reveal bullets one at a time as discussed
* Do not show all bullets at once
* Paragraph text appears all at once
* Bullet content appears progressively

**Emphasis & Attention**

* Reveal bullet points progressively in alignment with narration
* Do not display all bullet points simultaneously unless appropriate
* Display paragraph text in full when required for comprehension

## ****Editing & Pacing****

* Align slide transitions with natural breaks in narration
* Maintain pacing that allows time for comprehension
* Introduce meaningful visuals approximately every 60–90 seconds
* Avoid overbuilding checklists or sequences
* Monitor on-screen motion to ensure clarity and focus

## ****Visuals (Images, B-Roll, AI)****

### ****Visual & Image Standards****

* All visuals must support the concept being taught
* Do not include decorative or filler visuals
* Do not include visuals that are not referenced or explained
* Do not use distracting animations
* Highlight frameworks using underline or borders

### ****Use of B-Roll & Copyrighted Images****

* B-roll and imagery should be used to enhance understanding and provide relevant context
* Visuals should only be included when they contribute meaningfully to the learning experience

**When to Use**

* Illustrating real-world applications or scenarios
* Providing contextual grounding (e.g., industry, environment, processes)
* Supporting abstract or complex concepts

**When NOT to Use**

* When the concept is already clear without additional visuals
* When imagery does not directly relate to the content
* When visuals distract from key information

**Placement & Timing**

* Align b-roll directly with narration
* Do not overlap important text or visuals
* Ensure content is not blocked (e.g., progress bar)
* Use simple, consistent transitions

**Quality & Standards**

* Use high-quality, relevant images only
* Do not use generic or staged stock imagery
* Maintain consistency across videos

### ****Use of AI-Generated Images****

* AI-generated imagery may be used where it enhances clarity or supports conceptual understanding
* It should not be used as a substitute for purposeful design decisions

**Quality Requirements**

* All images must be **high resolution (minimum 4K: 3840 × 2160)**
* Images must be sharp and free of distortion
* Do not use images with warped text or unrealistic features

**When to Use**

* To visualize abstract concepts
* When stock imagery is insufficient
* When realism is not required

**When NOT to Use**

* When real-world imagery is more accurate
* When output is low quality or distracting
* When it introduces confusion

**Consistency**

* Maintain consistent visual style within a video
* Do not mix conflicting visual styles

## ****Designing for Learning****

* Reduce cognitive load
* Break content into small segments
* Guide learner attention
* Reinforce key ideas
* Support retention through summarization

**Technical Content (Equations, Data, etc.)**

* Include when referenced in narration
* Ensure readability
* Reveal step-by-step when possible
* Focus attention on relevant parts
* Do not remove for aesthetic reasons

## ****Video Text Content Rules****

* If faculty is speaking → show only key words or visuals
* Do not duplicate full spoken content on screen
* If no narration:
  + Allow time for reading (~200 WPM)
  + Ensure content is readable

## ****Video Production & Appearance****

* Thumbnails must use the Ivey logo
* Backgrounds must be consistent (provided standard)
* Add approved intro music

**Technical Checks**

* Ensure text is not blocked by progress bar
* Ensure video is not cut off on smaller screens

## ****Consistency & Quality Control****

* Ensure audio and visuals are fully synchronized
* Align slides with faculty narration
* Follow storyboard direction
* Confirm with learning designer when clarification is needed
* Maintain consistency in:
  + Video quality
  + Audio levels
  + Visual clarity

## ****Common Mistakes to Avoid****

* Too many bullets on one slide
* Full sentences copied from script
* Images added for decoration
* All content appearing at once
* Misalignment between audio and visuals
